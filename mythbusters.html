Mythbusters!

There is a lot of conflicting food fad and exercise trends out there at the moment. Never has there been a time where there has been more information available to parents and carers around what foods should be encouraged and what exercise is the best. Not all this information is true - much is anecdotal, sensational and can fall into the realm of 'pseudo-science'.

What this section aims to do is to dispel some of the common myths around food and exercise. We will do this by referring to proven and up-to-date scientific research, and links to our sources will be provided so you can do your own further research. Where there is a popular idea but little research - we will reference that too so you have all the information available. We will also include links to other sites that you may find of interest. Here we go!

The Food Pyramid vs The Food Plate Since 2011 there has been a shift from looking at the Food Pyramid as a guide to what we should eat to the Food Plate. the key difference is that fruits and vegetables should form half of a person's diet as opposed to breads and grains. Vegetables should form more than fruit, with grains forming slightly more than protein. Kids Health
